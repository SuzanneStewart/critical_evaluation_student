Level 6 Seminar: Using Critical Evaluation in Your Work

7 Example 6 excerpt

 

“In terms of parental involvement, parental components are well-established within child-focused CBT (Kaslow & Thompson, 2008) and have reduced symptoms by enhancing the effect on coping techniques (Mendlowitz et al., 1999). However parental involvement in CBT has not always been associated with significant improvement (Stallard, 2006), therefore a combination approach may be necessary. The combination of CBT and medication has been found to be more effective in the treatment of depression (O’Callaghan & Cunningham, 2015), however NICE guidelines (2015) state medication should only be prescribed in severe cases of depression and does not advise subscription to children.”
